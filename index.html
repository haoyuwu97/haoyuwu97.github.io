About

Research profile

My work sits at the interface of molecular simulation, polymer physics, and computational modeling. I am interested in building reliable and extensible software that connects atomistic or coarse-grained simulation outputs to physically interpretable observables and, when needed, to higher-level continuum descriptions.

Across the current codebase portfolio, the main themes are: force-field development for reactive systems, equilibrium and nonequilibrium analysis of simulation trajectories, crystallization and morphology characterization, viscoelastic linear-response analysis, and transport or electrostatic continuum models informed by simulation-derived kernels.

Software

Selected repositories

PILOTS

Trajectory analysis

High-performance, reproducible trajectory-analysis runner for large-scale molecular simulations with an extensible measure framework.

CHANNEL

Continuum modeling

1D continuum simulator for charge, ion, and redox physics in nanoscale-to-device workflows, designed to consume PILOTS-generated kernels.

IMPACT

Crystallization analysis

Coarse-grained crystallization analysis toolkit for semi-crystalline polymers and polymeric nanocomposites.

VELA

Linear response

Stress-autocorrelation and modulus-analysis package for converting MD stress time series into time- and frequency-domain viscoelastic observables.

pair_style-sw-as

LAMMPS extension

LAMMPS pair style implementing a modified Stillinger–Weber three-body potential for associative vitrimer bonding.

GNM

Monte Carlo

MC / GCMC package for simple Lennard–Jones particle systems.
